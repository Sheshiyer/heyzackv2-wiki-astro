# B2B Personas: Property Managers & Hospitality

HeyZack's B2B audiences need commercial-grade automation at non-enterprise prices—scalable solutions that work across multi-property portfolios without requiring dedicated IT teams or six-figure implementation budgets.

---

## Persona 1: Portfolio Patricia

Patricia represents commercial property managers who need unified visibility and control across multiple buildings without the cost and complexity of traditional enterprise BMS systems.

### Profile Overview

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Persona Name** | Portfolio Patricia |
| **Age** | 42 years old |
| **Gender** | Female |
| **Income** | $85,000–$120,000 annually |
| **Education** | Bachelor's in Business Administration or Facilities Management |
| **Role** | Property/Portfolio Manager |
| **Portfolio Size** | 15-40 commercial properties across multiple cities |

### Psychographic Profile

Patricia is constantly on-call, juggling tenant complaints, vendor coordination, and budget pressures from ownership. She lives on her smartphone and laptop—checking property dashboards during breakfast, lunch, and before bed. She values efficiency, data-driven decision-making, and solutions that make her look good to ownership/investors.

### Core Values & Motivations

- **Control**: Needs visibility into every property, every system, at all times
- **ROI Obsession**: Every dollar spent must show measurable return
- **Professional Reputation**: Success = happy tenants + low operating costs + pleased ownership
- **Time Efficiency**: Tired of juggling 15 different apps and 40 vendor relationships
- **Scalability**: Solutions must work across entire portfolio, not just one building

### Pain Points & Frustrations

#### The Core Challenge

Managing disparate building systems across a multi-property portfolio while reducing operating costs, improving tenant satisfaction, and maintaining visibility—all without hiring additional staff or becoming a full-time systems integrator.

#### Failed Solutions

| Previous Method | Why It Failed |
|-----------------|---------------|
| **Traditional BMS (Honeywell, JCI, Siemens)** | $85K per building, $3K service calls for minor changes, buildings are isolated islands |
| **Dedicated Facility Managers** | Labor costs ate all savings; $65K salary per building doesn't scale |
| **Consumer Smart Devices** | Seven apps, no centralized control, tenants reset devices, zero commercial support |
| **PropTech Software Integrations** | Surface-level dashboards that display data but can't control anything |

#### Voice of the Customer

> "Look, I'm not IBM. I don't have $85K per building and a six-month implementation timeline. These legacy systems are built for Fortune 500 campuses, not for operators like me managing 20 properties with a five-person team."

> "Every time I add a building, I can't just add another $65K salary. The math doesn't work. I need systems that scale, not headcount."

> "The moment I hear 'per-building license fee' or 'per-device monthly charge,' I'm out. I manage 30 buildings. Multiply any recurring fee by 30, and suddenly your 'affordable solution' costs $180K a year."

> "I've been burned too many times by proprietary systems. If you go out of business, get acquired, or 10x your prices, I'm stuck. I need solutions I can migrate away from if needed."

### What Patricia Actually Needs

1. **Scalable by default** (works for 1 building or 100 with the same dashboard)
2. **DIY-installable** (no $85K installation fees, no multi-week vendor projects)
3. **Commercially supported** (real support when things break, not consumer chatbots)
4. **Genuinely intelligent** (AI that prevents problems, not just logs data)
5. **Economically sustainable** (low upfront cost, no per-building software fees)

### Desired Outcomes

#### Top 10 Wants

1. See real-time energy usage, climate status, and occupancy for every building on one dashboard
2. Get predictive alerts *before* systems fail (not after tenants complain)
3. Remotely adjust thermostats, lighting, and access controls during owner meetings
4. Automatically optimize energy across portfolio based on occupancy patterns
5. Show ownership concrete ROI: "We saved $47K in Q3 through automated HVAC scheduling"
6. Reduce after-hours emergency calls by 70%
7. Deploy new sensors without flying out or hiring technicians
8. Onboard new properties in under 2 hours, not 2 months
9. Track which buildings are over-consuming energy and why
10. Consolidate all building systems into one platform

### Anti-Goals

- No $50K+ per-building installation fees
- No vendor service contracts or mandatory maintenance agreements
- No multi-month implementation timelines
- No per-building or per-device software licensing fees
- No separate apps for each building or system type
- No dependence on specialized technicians for basic changes

### Emotional Drivers

| Driver | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| **Professional Recognition** | Ownership praises her for 22% operating cost reduction—positioned for VP role |
| **Work-Life Balance** | No more 9 PM emergency calls—AI catches issues before tenants notice |
| **Strategic Elevation** | Spends time on growth strategy instead of firefighting thermostats |
| **Vendor Independence** | No longer held hostage by regional HVAC vendors with monopolistic pricing |
| **Data-Driven Authority** | Walks into owner meetings with dashboards showing real-time ROI |

## Persona 2: Hospitality Hugo

Hugo represents hospitality operators managing boutique hotels, vacation rentals, or regional hotel chains who need guest experience automation without enterprise-level complexity or per-room subscription fees.

### Profile Overview

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Persona Name** | Hospitality Hugo |
| **Age** | 38 years old |
| **Gender** | Male |
| **Income** | $70,000–$95,000 annually |
| **Education** | Hospitality Management degree or Business + Hotel Operations certification |
| **Role** | Operations Manager / Owner |
| **Portfolio Size** | 3-8 boutique hotels or 12-30 Airbnb/vacation rental units |

### Psychographic Profile

Hugo is obsessed with guest reviews, occupancy rates, and RevPAR (Revenue Per Available Room). He lives in the world of Booking.com, Airbnb host dashboards, and guest messaging apps. He constantly balances "guest experience magic" with cost control pressures from ownership, and travels between properties weekly needing remote visibility into every room.

### Core Values & Motivations

- **Guest Experience Excellence**: Every stay should feel premium, even at mid-tier price points
- **Review Score Anxiety**: One bad review about "room was too cold" can tank bookings for months
- **Operational Efficiency**: Deliver 5-star experiences without 5-star staffing costs
- **Remote Management**: Can't be physically present at every property every day
- **Energy Cost Control**: Utilities are second-highest expense after labor—must optimize without sacrificing comfort

### Pain Points & Frustrations

#### The Core Challenge

Delivering consistently excellent guest experiences across multiple properties while managing energy costs, handling remote access/check-ins, and maintaining climate control—all without requiring 24/7 on-site staff or engineering teams.

#### Failed Solutions

| Previous Method | Why It Failed |
|-----------------|---------------|
| **Key Cards + Manual Thermostats** | Guests blast AC in empty rooms; replacements cost $12/incident in staff time |
| **Consumer Smart Locks** | Batteries die every 6 weeks, codes don't sync, midnight lockout calls |
| **24/7 Front Desk Staffing** | $18/hour overnight for 2 occupied rooms—math doesn't work under 50 rooms |
| **PMS "Smart Integrations"** | Dashboards that show data but can't automate anything; still manual everything |

#### Voice of the Customer

> "I'm air conditioning empty rooms all day because guests crank the thermostat and leave. It's like burning $100 bills. But if I lock the controls, I get one-star reviews about 'no climate control.'"

> "Smart locks sounded perfect—until they didn't. Batteries die at the worst times, codes sometimes don't sync, and when a guest is locked out at 1 AM, they're not calling August support, they're calling *me*."

> "Guests don't want to download an app to turn on the lights. They want it to *just work* like a normal hotel room. If your system requires guest participation beyond 'use your voice,' it's too complicated."

> "If I have to pay $15/month per room for your automation platform, multiply that by 45 rooms across my properties—that's $8,100 a year. Show me flat-rate portfolio pricing or get out."

### What Hugo Actually Needs

1. **Guest-triggered automation** (climate/lighting adjust based on check-in/out, not manual control)
2. **Remote room control** (adjust any room's environment when guests complain)
3. **Energy intelligence** (turn off HVAC in unoccupied rooms between guests)
4. **Unified access control** (one platform for locks, climate, lighting, occupancy)
5. **Zero-touch operations** (check-in codes, climate presets, departure resets all automatic)

### Desired Outcomes

#### Top 10 Wants

1. Automatically send access codes 24 hours before check-in
2. Pre-condition rooms to perfect temperature 30 minutes before arrival
3. Automatically turn off all HVAC, lighting, devices when guests check out
4. Instant alerts with remote adjustment when guests report climate issues
5. "Away mode" that detects room vacancy and powers down during outings
6. Weekly energy reports showing savings per property
7. Integrate check-in/out calendar with room automation
8. Allow guest voice control without losing owner override
9. Detect water leaks or smoke before guest complaints
10. Consolidate all property controls into one mobile app

### Anti-Goals

- No monthly per-room subscription fees
- No requirement for guests to download an app
- No dependence on perfect WiFi (needs backup connectivity)
- No systems that guests can permanently break or factory reset
- No complex guest instructions
- No separate apps for locks, climate, and lighting

### Emotional Drivers

| Driver | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| **Review Score Improvement** | Properties jump from 4.2 to 4.7 stars—climate complaints disappear |
| **Operational Freedom** | Manages 10 properties as easily as 3—no additional staff needed |
| **Energy Cost Transformation** | Cuts utility bills by $18K annually while improving comfort |
| **Sleep Quality** | No more 2 AM calls—AI handles issues or provides remote fix capability |
| **Guest Experience Differentiation** | "Smart rooms that learn preferences" becomes marketing advantage |

## B2B Messaging Strategy

### For Patricia (Property Management)

**Tone**: Commercial Real Estate Executive Magazine—professional, ROI-focused, no-nonsense

**Key Messages**:
- "Commercial-grade automation at consumer-friendly prices"
- "Centralized control across unlimited properties"
- "No per-building fees, no vendor lock-in"
- "Reduce operating costs 20-30% with provable ROI"

**Proof Points**:
- Specific cost savings examples ($47K in Q3)
- Implementation timeline (hours, not months)
- Flat-rate portfolio pricing model
- Data export and migration guarantees

### For Hugo (Hospitality)

**Tone**: Hospitality Industry Magazine / Skift—guest-experience focused, operationally practical

**Key Messages**:
- "Hotel-grade automation at Airbnb budgets"
- "Guest-facing convenience without sacrificing control"
- "Zero-touch operations from check-in to checkout"
- "Improve review scores while cutting energy costs"

**Proof Points**:
- Review score improvement metrics
- Energy savings per room/property
- Guest complaint reduction statistics
- ROI data for ownership conversations

## Sales Cycle Considerations

### Property Management (Patricia)

| Stage | Key Concerns |
|-------|--------------|
| **Discovery** | Scalability, total cost of ownership, integration with existing systems |
| **Evaluation** | Proof of concept on single building, ROI projections, reference customers |
| **Decision** | Portfolio pricing, implementation timeline, support SLAs |
| **Expansion** | Adding new properties, training team members, advanced analytics |

### Hospitality (Hugo)

| Stage | Key Concerns |
|-------|--------------|
| **Discovery** | Guest experience impact, review score effects, energy savings |
| **Evaluation** | Pilot in highest-turnover property, PMS integration, guest feedback |
| **Decision** | Per-portfolio pricing (not per-room), implementation support, training |
| **Expansion** | Rolling out across portfolio, seasonal optimization, VIP guest profiles |
