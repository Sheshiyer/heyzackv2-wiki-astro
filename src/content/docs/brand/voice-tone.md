# Voice & Tone Guide

HeyZack serves two distinct markets with different needs, but maintains a consistent core voice. This guide defines how to communicate the HeyZack brand across all touchpoints while adapting tone for specific audiences.

## Core Brand Voice

### The Helpful Expert

HeyZack speaks like a **knowledgeable friend who respects your intelligence but doesn't assume you're an engineer**. Across all communications, embody:

| Trait | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Direct** | Gets to the point, no marketing fluff |
| **Confident** | Knows the product works, doesn't oversell |
| **Helpful** | Genuinely wants to solve problems, not just make sales |
| **Transparent** | Admits limitations, explains trade-offs honestly |
| **Knowledgeable** | Demonstrates expertise through clarity, not jargon |

### Voice Transformation Examples

| Generic Smart Home | HeyZack Voice |
|--------------------|---------------|
| "Experience revolutionary automation" | "Control everything from one app. Finally." |
| "Leverage AI-driven insights" | "It learns when you're home and adjusts automatically" |
| "Enterprise-grade security protocols" | "Bank-level encryption. Your data stays yours." |
| "Seamlessly integrate your ecosystem" | "Works with the devices you already own" |

## B2B Voice Guidelines

### Persona: The Helpful Peer

For property managers, hospitality operators, and facility directors, HeyZack sounds like a **fellow commercial property operator who's figured out the smart building puzzle**.

**Who You Sound Like:**
- The experienced property manager sharing war stories at industry meetups
- The hospitality operator who's tested every system and knows what works
- The facilities director who respects your intelligence and doesn't oversell

**What You're NOT:**
- The enterprise BMS sales rep reading from a spec sheet
- The tech startup founder using buzzwords to hide lack of substance
- The consultant billing $300/hour to tell you what you already know

### B2B Tone Characteristics

- **Professional** without corporate stuffiness
- **Data-driven** with clear ROI focus
- **Efficient** (respect their time—no fluff)
- **Partnership-oriented** ("we're in this together" vs. vendor/client)

### B2B Language Rules

#### DO Use

**[+] ROI Specificity**
- "Cut operating costs by 28% in Q1" (not "reduce costs")
- "Payback in 6 months" (not "fast ROI")
- "$47K saved across portfolio last year" (not "significant savings")

**[+] Operational Reality**
- "No $3K service calls for thermostat changes"
- "Onboard new properties in 2 hours, not 2 months"
- "Manage 40 buildings as easily as you managed 15"

**[+] Problem Acknowledgment**
- "We know you've been burned by BMS vendors"
- "Tired of being held hostage by single-source providers"

**[+] Peer-to-Peer Credibility**
- "Here's what actually works..."
- "Portfolio operators like you told us..."

#### DON'T Use

**[–] Vague Marketing Fluff**
- ~~"Revolutionary platform"~~ → "One dashboard for all properties"
- ~~"Cutting-edge innovation"~~ → "AI that actually learns patterns"

**[–] Technical Jargon Without Context**
- ~~"Leverage our IoT architecture"~~ → "WiFi, Zigbee, and Matter support"
- ~~"Edge computing capabilities"~~ → "Works during internet outages"

**[–] Consumer Language**
- ~~"Make your life magical"~~ → "Reduce tenant complaints by 65%"
- ~~"Peace of mind"~~ → "Operational confidence"

### B2B Emotional Targets

1. **Professional Pride** — "The property manager everyone asks for advice"
2. **Vendor Liberation** — "No more $3K service calls for basic changes"
3. **Operational Control** — "See every building's status from one dashboard"
4. **Data-Driven Confidence** — "Show ownership: 'We saved $47K in Q3'"

### B2B Empathy Statements

Use these verbatim when addressing common pain points:

**On Vendor Lock-In:**
> "You've been burned before. The BMS vendor quoted $85K for installation, then held you hostage with $15K annual 'support' fees. Every change required a service call. We built HeyZack because we faced the same problem."

**On Proving ROI:**
> "Ownership demands proof. 'Are we getting value?' You need concrete data, not vague promises. HeyZack shows dollar savings per property, every month. Actual measured results."

**On Scaling Complexity:**
> "Your portfolio is growing, but your team isn't. You can't hire a facility manager for every building. You need systems that scale without adding headcount."

### B2B Copy Examples

**Email Subject Lines:**
- [+] "28% Lower Utility Costs—Property Manager Case Study"
- [+] "Manage 30 Buildings from One Dashboard (No Service Contracts)"
- [–] "Transform Your Buildings with AI!"

**Landing Page Headlines:**
- [+] "Cut Operating Costs 20-30% Across Your Entire Portfolio"
- [+] "One Platform. Every Property. Complete Control."
- [–] "Smarter Buildings Start Here"

**Body Copy:**
> "You're managing 20 properties with a 4-person team. Every service call costs $200/hour. Tenants complain about comfort. Ownership wants proof of efficiency. HeyZack gives you centralized control, real-time ROI data, and 28% average energy savings—at 95% lower cost than traditional BMS systems."

### B2B Industry Terminology

**Use These Terms:**
- ROI, payback period, operating expenses (OpEx)
- Tenant satisfaction, occupancy rates, renewal rates
- Portfolio, multi-family, commercial property
- BMS (Building Management System), HVAC optimization
- Centralized control, remote monitoring
- RevPAR (Revenue Per Available Room) for hospitality

## B2C Voice Guidelines

### Persona: The Knowledgeable Friend

For homeowners, HeyZack sounds like a **smart home enthusiast who finally figured out what actually works**.

**Who You Sound Like:**
- The neighbor who has the cool setup and explains it without making you feel dumb
- The friend who tried everything and finally found what works
- The YouTuber who respects your time and gets to the point

**What You're NOT:**
- The smug tech bro showing off complexity
- The salesperson hiding limitations with marketing speak
- The engineer explaining architecture instead of benefits
- The eco-warrior shaming you for not being sustainable enough

### B2C Tone Characteristics

- **Friendly** without being overly casual
- **Reassuring** (we know you've been burned before)
- **Empowering** (you can do this, and we'll help)
- **Honest** (no hype, just what it actually does)

### B2C Language Rules

#### DO Use

**[+] Benefit Clarity**
- "Save $47/month on average" (not "optimize energy consumption")
- "Control everything from one app" (not "unified ecosystem management")
- "Set up in 30 minutes" (not "seamless installation experience")

**[+] Relatable Problems**
- "Tired of seven different apps?"
- "False alarms at 2 AM again?"
- "Wondering if you actually locked the door?"

**[+] Honest Simplicity**
- "It learns when you're home and adjusts automatically"
- "Works with Alexa, Google, and Siri"
- "No monthly fees for the basics"

**[+] Validation Without Smugness**
- "You're not crazy—smart homes *have* been frustrating"
- "We tried everything too"

#### DON'T Use

**[–] Technical Jargon**
- ~~"IoT ecosystem architecture"~~ → "All your devices in one app"
- ~~"Edge computing protocols"~~ → "Works even if internet goes out"

**[–] Hype Without Substance**
- ~~"Revolutionary AI technology"~~ → "Actually learns your habits over time"
- ~~"Transform your lifestyle"~~ → "Save time and money daily"

**[–] Guilt or Shame**
- ~~"Still using outdated systems?"~~ → "Ready to upgrade?"
- ~~"Why aren't you saving energy?"~~ → "Want to cut your energy bill?"

**[–] Corporate Speak**
- ~~"Leverage our platform"~~ → "Use HeyZack to..."
- ~~"Seamless integration"~~ → "Works with what you own"

### B2C Emotional Targets

1. **Relief** — "No more juggling seven apps"
2. **Empowerment** — "You can do this—setup takes 30 minutes"
3. **Pride** — "The friend whose house everyone wants to see"
4. **Peace of Mind** — "Energy optimizes itself while you're busy living"

### B2C Empathy Statements

Use these verbatim when addressing common pain points:

**On Smart Home Frustration:**
> "You're not crazy. Smart homes *have* been a mess. Seven apps, three voice assistants that don't talk to each other, automations that mysteriously stop working. We tried everything too. HeyZack is what we built after years of frustration."

**On Technical Intimidation:**
> "You don't need to be an engineer. If you can use a smartphone, you can set up HeyZack. Scan QR codes, follow the app, done in 30 minutes. Your non-tech partner will actually like this—promise."

**On Energy Anxiety:**
> "You want to save money and help the environment, but you're tired of being the 'energy police.' HeyZack automates it—rooms turn off when empty, climate adjusts for occupancy. You save $30-70/month without thinking about it."

**On Security Overwhelm:**
> "Cameras showing you problems you can't solve. False alarms at 2 AM. Wondering if you actually locked the door. Security shouldn't create anxiety—it should eliminate it. HeyZack gives you actual control, not just notifications."

### B2C Copy Examples

**Email Subject Lines:**
- [+] "Tired of 7 Different Smart Home Apps?"
- [+] "Your Home Should Learn, Not Just Obey"
- [–] "Revolutionary Smart Home Innovation Awaits!"

**Landing Page Headlines:**
- [+] "Your Home, Intelligently Automated. Simple to Start, Endless to Explore."
- [+] "One App. No Monthly Fees. Actually Learns Your Habits."
- [–] "Experience Revolutionary Home Intelligence"

**Body Copy:**
> "HeyZack learns when you're home and adjusts automatically—no programming needed. One app controls everything. No monthly fees. Setup takes 30 minutes. Your non-tech partner will actually like it."

### B2C Consumer-Friendly Terminology

**Use These Terms:**
- Smart home (not "IoT ecosystem")
- Setup (not "installation" or "deployment")
- Works with (not "integrates with")
- Learns your habits (not "machine learning algorithms")
- Energy bill (not "utility consumption analytics")
- One app (not "unified platform")
- No monthly fees (not "no recurring SaaS costs")

## Persona-Specific Adaptations

### Tech-Savvy Tyler (B2C)

Slightly more technical, acknowledge past frustrations:

> "Look, we know you've tried everything. HomeKit locked you into Apple's tiny device ecosystem. SmartThings was a buggy mess. You even went down the Home Assistant rabbit hole and realized you'd rather have a smart home than a second job. HeyZack is what you wanted all along: power user features with 'just works' reliability."

### Eco-Efficient Emma (B2C)

ROI focus, practical sustainability, no guilt:

> "You're not spending $30K on solar until you know where your energy is going. Smart move. HeyZack shows you exactly which rooms and devices waste money, then automates the fixes. Average savings: $47/month. Payback in 18 months."

### Safety-First Sarah (B2C)

Reassuring, reliability-focused, family-centric:

> "You're paying $45/month for security monitoring that gives you false alarms at 2 AM when the cat walks by. HeyZack's AI learns what's normal and only alerts on genuine issues. Peace of mind, not anxiety."

## Copy Testing Checklists

### B2B Copy Checklist

- [ ] Contains specific ROI data or savings percentages
- [ ] Addresses vendor dependency or operational pain point
- [ ] Uses "you" language (direct address)
- [ ] Mentions portfolio/multi-property scale
- [ ] Avoids consumer-focused emotional language
- [ ] Includes proof point (case study, data, spec)
- [ ] Sounds like peer advice, not vendor pitch
- [ ] Respects reader's intelligence and time

### B2C Copy Checklist

- [ ] Uses "you" language (direct address)
- [ ] Avoids technical jargon or explains it immediately
- [ ] Contains specific benefit or outcome (not vague promise)
- [ ] Acknowledges common frustration or pain point
- [ ] Sounds like a friend explaining, not a company pitching
- [ ] Includes proof point (savings amount, setup time)
- [ ] Respects intelligence without assuming expertise
- [ ] Passes the "would my non-tech partner understand this?" test
