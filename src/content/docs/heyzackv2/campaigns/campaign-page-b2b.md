You're managing 20 properties with a 5-person team. 

It's 9 PM on Friday. Your phone rings. Again. Tenant in Building F: "The heat isn't working." You log into the BMS system—if you can remember the password for that specific building. The vendor-installed system requires a $200/hour after-hours service call just to adjust a thermostat remotely.

Or maybe you tried the "smart" route. Consumer-grade Nest thermostats, Ring cameras, smart locks. Seemed cheaper upfront. Now you're juggling seven different apps, tenants keep factory-resetting devices, and when something breaks, you're chatting with a consumer support bot that tells you to "restart the device."

**Meanwhile, ownership wants answers:**

You know the data exists—energy consumption, occupancy patterns, system failures—but it's trapped in disconnected systems. You're flying blind with a $2.4M annual operating budget.
