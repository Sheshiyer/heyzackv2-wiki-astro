# Press Release

## Headline
HeyZack Launches AI-Powered Automation Platform That Cuts Building Costs 95% While Serving Both Homes and Commercial Properties

## Subhead
Dual-market smart building system delivers 20-30% energy savings with DIY installation, eliminating vendor lock-in for residential homeowners and portfolio-scale property managers alike

## Dateline
SAN FRANCISCO, CA, February 1, 2026 — HeyZack today announced the launch of its AI-powered smart home and commercial building automation platform that disrupts both the $80 billion residential smart home market and the $95 billion commercial building management system (BMS) industry. Unlike competitors focused on a single market segment, HeyZack's 128-product ecosystem scales seamlessly from individual homes to unlimited commercial property portfolios—at 95% lower cost than traditional enterprise BMS systems while delivering genuine AI learning instead of rule-based scheduling.

The platform addresses a critical market gap: homeowners struggle with fragmented ecosystems requiring seven or more apps to control different devices, while property managers face vendor lock-in with traditional BMS providers charging $50,000-$85,000 per building plus mandatory service contracts. HeyZack's unified solution delivers portfolio-scale management for $5,000 per property with DIY installation and zero vendor dependencies, while residential customers gain one-app control of their entire smart home without monthly subscriptions.

## Product Overview

HeyZack's comprehensive ecosystem includes 128 products across 15 categories:

- 2K/1080p cameras with night vision and solar power options
- Smart sensors for motion/door/window/environmental monitoring
- Control panels with built-in Alexa and Zigbee gateways
- Smart switches and DIY circuit breakers with power monitoring
- Door locks with face recognition and video integration
- Climate control systems including thermostats and radiator valves
- Smart lighting with RGB and dimming capabilities
- Motorized curtain systems
- Smart home accessories

### Key Technical Specifications

| Feature | Specification |
|---------|---------------|
| **Connectivity** | WiFi (2.4GHz/5GHz), Zigbee 3.0, Bluetooth BLE/Mesh, Matter protocol, IR (38KHz) |
| **Power Options** | Battery, solar (6W panels), hardwired (AC 100-240V), USB-C |
| **Weather Resistance** | IP65 outdoor, IP20 indoor, -20°C to 60°C operating range |
| **Platform Compatibility** | Tuya Smart/Smart Life, Amazon Alexa, Google Home, Apple HomeKit (via Matter) |
| **Security** | Bank-level encryption, local storage options, edge computing |
| **Languages** | 16-language support |

## Key Benefits

### For Commercial Property Managers
- Portfolio-scale centralized control across unlimited properties from one dashboard
- 20-30% energy cost reduction with AI-driven HVAC and lighting optimization
- Real-time ROI tracking with transparent dollar savings per property
- Zero vendor lock-in: DIY installation, no service contracts, open protocol architecture
- Automated tenant/guest experiences (check-in codes, climate pre-conditioning, access control)

### For Residential Homeowners
- One unified app controls all 128 product types (vs. 7+ fragmented apps)
- Genuine AI learning that improves over time (not rule-based "if-then" automation)
- 15-30% energy bill savings ($360-$720/year average) with whole-home monitoring
- No monthly subscriptions for core features (optional cloud storage only)
- 30-minute DIY setup per device, no electrician required
- Universal device compatibility (works with existing Nest, Ring, Philips Hue, etc.)

## Market Opportunity

The global smart home market is projected to reach $174 billion by 2025 (Statista), while the commercial building automation market is forecast at $121 billion by 2026 (MarketsandMarkets).

### Residential Market Pain Points
- 73% of smart home owners report frustration with fragmented ecosystems (Parks Associates, 2025)
- Average smart home household uses 6.8 different device brands with 5.2 separate control apps (Strategy Analytics, 2024)
- Only 31% of smart thermostats deliver advertised energy savings due to poor integration (ACEEE, 2024)

### Commercial Market Pain Points
- Traditional BMS installation costs $450-$850 per square foot (Navigant Research, 2025)
- 68% of property managers report vendor lock-in as #1 frustration (BOMA, 2025)
- Average commercial building wastes 30% of energy due to outdated HVAC systems (DOE, 2024)

**Total Addressable Market:** $295 billion
