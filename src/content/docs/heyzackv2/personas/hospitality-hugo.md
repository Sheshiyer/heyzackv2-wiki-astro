# Buyer Persona Dossier

## 1. Executive Summary

**Persona Name:** Hospitality Hugo

**Demographics:** 38 years old | Male | $70,000-$95,000 annual income | Hospitality Management degree or Business + Hotel Operations certification

**Psychographics:**
- Manages 3-8 boutique hotels, Airbnb portfolio (12-30 units), or operations for regional hotel chain
- Obsessed with guest reviews, occupancy rates, and RevPAR
- Lives in the world of Booking.com, Airbnb host dashboards, and guest messaging apps
- Constantly balancing "guest experience magic" with cost control pressures
- Travels between properties weekly, needs remote visibility into every room

**Values & Motivations:**
- **Guest Experience Excellence:** Every stay should feel premium
- **Review Score Anxiety:** One bad review can tank bookings for months
- **Operational Efficiency:** Needs 5-star experiences without 5-star staffing costs
- **Remote Management:** Can't be physically present at every property every day
- **Energy Cost Control:** Utilities are second-highest expense after labor

## 2. The Struggle & Failed Methods

### The Main Challenge
Delivering consistently excellent guest experiences across multiple properties while managing energy costs, handling remote access/check-ins, and maintaining control of in-room climate/lighting—without requiring 24/7 on-site staff.

### Previous Methods & Frustrations

**Method: Traditional Hotel Key Card Systems + Manual Thermostats**
> "Guests leave the AC running at 62°F all day even when they're out. We're literally air conditioning empty rooms for 8 hours. But if we lock the thermostats, we get reviews complaining about 'no climate control.' And key cards? Half our guests lose them."

**Method: Smart Locks with Per-Guest Access Codes**
> "I thought smart locks would solve everything—just text the guest a code. Except the locks eat batteries every six weeks, the codes sometimes don't sync, and when a guest gets locked out at midnight, *I'm* the one answering the phone."

**Method: Hiring Night Staff or Front Desk Coverage**
> "For boutique hotels and vacation rentals, 24/7 staffing makes no financial sense. I'm paying someone $18/hour to sit at a desk from 11 PM to 7 AM when we have *two occupied rooms*."

**Method: Property Management Software with "Smart Integration"**
> "These platforms promise 'smart home integration,' but it's all surface-level. They'll import my thermostat data, but they can't actually *automate* anything useful."

## 3. Root Cause Analysis

Hugo, here's the brutal truth: **Hospitality tech has been designed for massive hotel chains with engineering departments**—not for operators like you managing boutique properties.

- Traditional systems assumed you'd have front desk staff and engineering on-call
- Consumer smart locks were built for homeowners, not high-turnover commercial use
- Property management software are *booking* tools pretending to be automation platforms

**What you actually need is:**
1. **Guest-triggered automation** (climate/lighting adjust based on check-in/out)
2. **Remote room control** (adjust any room's environment from your phone)
3. **Energy intelligence** (automatically turn off HVAC in unoccupied rooms)
4. **Unified access control** (one platform for locks, climate, lighting, occupancy)
5. **Zero-touch operations** (check-in codes, climate presets, departure resets all automatic)
