# Buyer Persona Dossier

## 1. Executive Summary

**Persona Name:** Portfolio Patricia

**Demographics:** 42 years old | Female | $85,000-$120,000 annual income | Bachelor's in Business Administration or Facilities Management

**Psychographics:** 
- Manages 15-40 commercial properties across multiple cities
- Constantly on-call, juggling tenant complaints, vendor coordination, and budget pressures
- Lives on her smartphone and laptop—checks property dashboards during breakfast, lunch, and before bed
- Values efficiency, data-driven decision-making, and solutions that make her look good to ownership
- Frustrated by systems that don't talk to each other

**Values & Motivations:**
- **Control:** Needs visibility into every property, every system, at all times
- **ROI Obsession:** Every dollar spent must show measurable return
- **Professional Reputation:** Success = happy tenants + low operating costs + pleased ownership
- **Time Efficiency:** Tired of juggling 15 different apps and 40 vendor relationships
- **Scalability:** Solutions must work across entire portfolio

---

## 2. The Struggle & Failed Methods

### The Main Challenge
Managing disparate building systems across a multi-property portfolio while reducing operating costs, improving tenant satisfaction, and maintaining visibility without hiring additional staff.

### Previous Methods & Frustrations

**Method: Traditional BMS (Honeywell, Johnson Controls, Siemens)**
> "We paid $85,000 per building for these systems, and I still can't see real-time data without calling a technician. Every time we want to add a new sensor or change something, it's a $3,000 service call. And forget about integrating buildings—each one is its own island."

**Method: Hiring Property-Specific Facility Managers**
> "We tried putting a dedicated manager in each building, but labor costs ate up any savings. Then we tried outsourcing to a facilities management company, and they just became another vendor I have to manage. I'm still the one answering owner calls at 9 PM."

**Method: Patchwork of Consumer Smart Home Devices**
> "We tried using off-the-shelf consumer products to save money, but now I have seven different apps, three different accounts, and when something breaks, there's no commercial support. Plus, tenants kept resetting devices. It was chaos."

**Method: Property Management Software with "Smart Building" Modules**
> "These property management apps all claim they're 'smart building ready' now, but it's smoke and mirrors. They'll show you data from your existing systems—great, but I'm still paying Honeywell $40K a year."

---

## 3. Root Cause Analysis

Patricia, here's why all those approaches left you exhausted: **Traditional BMS systems were built for massive corporate campuses with dedicated IT teams and unlimited budgets**—not for multi-property portfolios run by lean teams.

- They're insanely expensive to install, modify, and maintain
- They lock you into single-vendor ecosystems
- Hiring more people just shifted the problem
- Consumer smart home route had no scalability or accountability

**What you actually need is:**
1. **Scalable by default** (works for 1 building or 100 with the same dashboard)
2. **DIY-installable** (no $85K installation fees)
3. **Commercially supported** (real support when things break)
4. **Genuinely intelligent** (AI that learns patterns and prevents problems)
5. **Economically sustainable** (low upfront cost, no recurring per-building fees)
