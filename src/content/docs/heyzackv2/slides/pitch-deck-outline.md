# Pitch Deck Outline

Slide-by-slide structure for HeyZack investor and partner presentations.

---

## Investor Deck (12-15 Slides)

### Slide 1: Title
- **HeyZack** logo
- Tagline: "AI-Powered Building Automation for Everyone"
- Presenter name, date

### Slide 2: The Problem
- **Consumer pain:** 7 apps, false alarms, no real intelligence
- **Commercial pain:** $85K BMS systems, vendor lock-in, no ROI visibility
- **Market gap:** Nothing bridges consumer simplicity with commercial scale

### Slide 3: The Solution
- One unified platform for homes AND commercial properties
- AI that actually learns (not just schedules)
- DIY installation, no subscriptions, open protocols

### Slide 4: Product Demo
- App screenshot or video embed
- 3-4 key features highlighted
- "See it in action" approach

### Slide 5: Market Opportunity
- **TAM:** $150B global smart building market (2026)
- **SAM:** $45B residential + small commercial
- **SOM:** $2B DIY-installable segment
- Growth trajectory charts

### Slide 6: Business Model
- **Hardware margins:** 40-50% gross margin
- **Optional subscriptions:** Cloud storage, premium analytics
- **B2B tiers:** Portfolio licensing, white-label
- Unit economics breakdown

### Slide 7: Traction
- Pre-launch waitlist numbers
- Beta tester results (energy savings %, satisfaction)
- Partnership LOIs
- Press/media coverage

### Slide 8: Competitive Landscape
- 2x2 matrix: Simplicity vs. Intelligence
- HeyZack positioned in upper-right quadrant
- Key differentiators vs. Nest, SmartThings, Honeywell

### Slide 9: Go-to-Market
- **Phase 1:** Crowdfunding launch (awareness, validation)
- **Phase 2:** D2C e-commerce (Amazon, website)
- **Phase 3:** B2B channel partners (property management, hospitality)

### Slide 10: Team
- Founder photos and 1-line bios
- Relevant experience highlights
- Advisory board (if applicable)

### Slide 11: Financials
- 3-year revenue projection
- Path to profitability
- Key assumptions

### Slide 12: The Ask
- Funding amount sought
- Use of funds breakdown
- Milestones funding will achieve

### Slide 13: Vision
- "Imagine a world where every building optimizes itself"
- Long-term platform potential
- Closing inspiration

### Slide 14: Contact
- Email, website, social handles
- QR code to waitlist/demo

## Slide Design Notes

### Visual Hierarchy
1. **One idea per slide** — Don't overcrowd
2. **Headlines tell the story** — Readable in 3 seconds
3. **Data visualization** — Charts over tables
4. **Whitespace** — Let content breathe

### Brand Consistency
- Use HeyZack blue (#243984) for headers
- Use HeyZack pink (#E82F89) for emphasis
- Consistent font sizes across deck
- Logo on every slide (subtle, corner placement)

### Presentation Tips
- **10/20/30 rule:** 10 slides, 20 minutes, 30pt minimum font
- **Practice transitions:** Know what comes next
- **Anticipate questions:** Have backup slides ready
- **Energy matters:** Your enthusiasm sells as much as content
