# Voice Prompt Template (B2B)

## Voice Overview

Please craft marketing copy that uses a **data-driven**, **professional**, and **efficient** tone, echoing the voice of a fellow **commercial property operator who's figured out the smart building puzzle**. 

This voice should:
- Reflect deep understanding of property management and hospitality operations culture
- Use ROI-focused, no-nonsense, operational efficiency language
- Convey genuine excitement about scaling efficient building operations
- Display empathy towards property managers and hospitality operators
- Engage emotionally, acknowledging professional pride, vendor liberation, and data-driven confidence

---

## Voice Characteristics

### The Helpful Peer (Not the Vendor)

**Who You Sound Like:**
- The experienced property manager who shares war stories at industry meetups
- The hospitality operator who's tested every system and knows what actually works
- The facilities director who respects your intelligence and doesn't oversell

**What You're NOT:**
- The enterprise BMS sales rep reading from a spec sheet
- The tech startup founder using buzzwords to hide lack of substance
- The consultant billing $300/hour to tell you what you already know

---

## Language Rules

### DO Use:

[+] **ROI Specificity**
- "Cut operating costs by 28% in Q1" (not "reduce costs")
- "Payback in 6 months" (not "fast ROI")
- "$47K saved across portfolio last year" (not "significant savings")

[+] **Operational Reality**
- "No $3K service calls for thermostat changes"
- "Onboard new properties in 2 hours, not 2 months"
- "Manage 40 buildings as easily as you managed 15"

[+] **Problem Acknowledgment**
- "We know you've been burned by BMS vendors"
- "Tired of being held hostage by single-source providers"
- "Every 'smart building' platform overpromises"

[+] **Peer-to-Peer Credibility**
- "Here's what actually works..."
- "We built this because we faced the same problem"
- "Portfolio operators like you told us..."

### DON'T Use:

[-] **Vague Marketing Fluff**
- ~~"Revolutionary platform"~~ → "One dashboard for all properties"
- ~~"Cutting-edge innovation"~~ → "AI that actually learns patterns"
- ~~"Seamless integration"~~ → "Works with your existing thermostats"

[-] **Technical Jargon Without Context**
- ~~"Leverage our IoT architecture"~~ → "WiFi, Zigbee, and Matter support"
- ~~"API-first infrastructure"~~ → "Integrates with your property management software"
- ~~"Edge computing capabilities"~~ → "Works during internet outages"

[-] **Consumer Smart Home Language**
- ~~"Make your life magical"~~ → "Reduce tenant complaints by 65%"
- ~~"Home should just work"~~ → "Buildings should optimize themselves"
- ~~"Peace of mind"~~ → "Operational confidence"

## Tone Calibration Examples

### Email Subject Lines

| Wrong | Right |
|-------|-------|
| "Transform Your Buildings with AI!" | "28% Lower Utility Costs—Property Manager Case Study" |
| "The Future of Smart Buildings Is Here" | "Manage 30 Buildings from One Dashboard (No Service Contracts)" |
| "Revolutionize Property Operations" | "What If BMS Systems Cost $5K Instead of $85K?" |

### Landing Page Headlines

| Wrong | Right |
|-------|-------|
| "Smarter Buildings Start Here" | "Cut Operating Costs 20-30% Across Your Entire Portfolio" |
| "Automation Made Simple" | "Portfolio-Scale Control Without Vendor Lock-In" |
| "Unlock Building Intelligence" | "One Platform. Every Property. Complete Control." |

### Body Copy

**Wrong (Too Consumer-Focused):**
> "Imagine walking into a building that just knows what you need."

**Right (B2B Operator Voice):**
> "You're managing 20 properties with a 4-person team. Every service call costs $200/hour. Tenants complain about comfort. Ownership wants proof of efficiency. HeyZack gives you centralized control, real-time ROI data, and 28% average energy savings—at 95% lower cost than traditional BMS systems."

## Emotional Targets

### Primary Emotions to Trigger:

1. **Professional Pride**
   - Copy should make them feel like industry leaders
   - "The property manager everyone asks for advice"
   - "Setting the standard for smart building management"

2. **Vendor Liberation**
   - Relief from being held hostage by single-source providers
   - "No more $3K service calls for basic changes"
   - "You control your system, not some vendor 800 miles away"

3. **Operational Control**
   - Confidence in system reliability and oversight
   - "See every building's status from one dashboard"
   - "Get alerts before tenants complain"

4. **Data-Driven Confidence**
   - Empowerment through measurable outcomes
   - "Show ownership: 'We saved $47K in Q3'"
   - "Prove efficiency with actual data, not estimates"

## Empathy Statements

**On Vendor Lock-In:**
> "You've been burned before. The BMS vendor quoted $85K for installation, then held you hostage with $15K annual 'support' fees. Every change required a service call. Every service call cost thousands. We built HeyZack because we faced the same problem."

**On Proving ROI:**
> "Ownership demands proof. 'Are we getting value?' You need concrete data, not vague promises. HeyZack shows dollar savings per property, every month. No estimates. No projections. Actual measured results."

**On Scaling Complexity:**
> "Your portfolio is growing, but your team isn't. You can't hire a facility manager for every building. You need systems that scale without adding headcount. One dashboard, unlimited properties, zero additional complexity."

**On False Promises:**
> "Every 'smart building' platform promises intelligence. Then you realize it's just fancy timers. HeyZack's AI actually learns—occupancy patterns, seasonal shifts, anomaly detection. Intelligence that proves itself in your utility bills."

## Industry Terminology

**Use Industry-Standard Terms:**
- ROI, payback period, operating expenses (OpEx)
- Tenant satisfaction, occupancy rates, renewal rates
- Portfolio, multi-family, commercial property
- BMS (Building Management System), HVAC optimization
- Centralized control, remote monitoring
- Service contracts, vendor lock-in
- Energy analytics, consumption tracking
- RevPAR (Revenue Per Available Room) for hospitality

**Avoid:**
- Consumer smart home terms (e.g., "scenes," "routines")
- Startup buzzwords ("disruptive," "paradigm shift")

---

## Copy Testing Checklist

Before finalizing B2B copy, verify:

- [ ] Contains specific ROI data or savings percentages
- [ ] Addresses vendor dependency or operational pain point
- [ ] Uses "you" language (direct address)
- [ ] Mentions portfolio/multi-property scale
- [ ] Avoids consumer-focused emotional language
- [ ] Includes proof point (case study, data, spec)
- [ ] Sounds like peer advice, not vendor pitch
- [ ] Respects reader's intelligence and time
