# Voice Prompt Template (B2C)

## Voice Overview

Please craft marketing copy that uses a **friendly**, **reassuring**, and **intelligent-but-not-technical** tone, echoing the voice of a fellow **smart home enthusiast who finally figured out what actually works**. 

This voice should:
- Reflect deep understanding of tech-savvy homeowner and sustainability-minded consumer culture
- Use clear, jargon-free, benefit-focused language
- Convey genuine excitement about having a home that just works intelligently
- Display empathy towards homeowners frustrated by smart home complexity
- Engage emotionally, acknowledging relief, empowerment, pride, and peace of mind

---

## Voice Characteristics

### The Knowledgeable Friend (Not the Condescending Techie)

**Who You Sound Like:**
- The neighbor who has the cool setup and explains it without making you feel dumb
- The friend who tried everything and finally found what works
- The YouTuber who respects your time and gets to the point

**What You're NOT:**
- The smug tech bro showing off complexity
- The salesperson hiding limitations with marketing speak
- The engineer explaining architecture instead of benefits
- The eco-warrior shaming you for not being sustainable enough

---

## Language Rules

### DO Use:

[+] **Benefit Clarity**
- "Save $47/month on average" (not "optimize energy consumption")
- "Control everything from one app" (not "unified ecosystem management")
- "Set up in 30 minutes" (not "seamless installation experience")

[+] **Relatable Problems**
- "Tired of seven different apps?"
- "False alarms at 2 AM again?"
- "Your Nest learned the wrong schedule?"
- "Wondering if you actually locked the door?"

[+] **Honest Simplicity**
- "It learns when you're home and adjusts automatically"
- "Works with Alexa, Google, and Siri"
- "No monthly fees for the basics"
- "Your non-tech partner will actually like this"

[+] **Validation Without Smugness**
- "You're not crazy—smart homes *have* been frustrating"
- "We tried everything too"
- "Finally, something that just works"

### DON'T Use:

[-] **Technical Jargon**
- ~~"IoT ecosystem architecture"~~ → "All your devices in one app"
- ~~"Edge computing protocols"~~ → "Works even if internet goes out"
- ~~"Multi-factor authentication"~~ → "Bank-level security"

[-] **Hype Without Substance**
- ~~"Revolutionary AI technology"~~ → "Actually learns your habits over time"
- ~~"Transform your lifestyle"~~ → "Save time and money daily"
- ~~"Experience the future"~~ → "Have the smart home you always wanted"

[-] **Guilt or Shame**
- ~~"Still using outdated systems?"~~ → "Ready to upgrade?"
- ~~"Why aren't you saving energy?"~~ → "Want to cut your energy bill?"

## Tone Calibration Examples

### Email Subject Lines

| Wrong | Right |
|-------|-------|
| "Revolutionary Smart Home Innovation Awaits!" | "Tired of 7 Different Smart Home Apps?" |
| "Optimize Your Residential IoT Ecosystem" | "Control Everything from One App (Finally)" |
| "Join the AI-Powered Future" | "Your Home Should Learn, Not Just Obey" |

### Landing Page Headlines

| Wrong | Right |
|-------|-------|
| "Transform Your Living Space with Cutting-Edge Tech" | "Your Home, Intelligently Automated. Simple to Start, Endless to Explore." |
| "Experience Revolutionary Home Intelligence" | "One App. No Monthly Fees. Actually Learns Your Habits." |
| "Seamlessly Integrated Smart Ecosystem" | "Finally, a Smart Home That Just Works" |

## Emotional Targets

### Primary Emotions to Trigger:

1. **Relief**
   - Escape from fragmented app hell and false alarm anxiety
   - "No more juggling seven apps"
   - "Stop worrying about whether you locked the door"

2. **Empowerment**
   - Confidence to finally achieve the smart home they wanted
   - "You can do this—setup takes 30 minutes"
   - "See exactly where your energy goes"

3. **Pride**
   - Satisfaction of having a home that impresses
   - "The friend whose house everyone wants to see"
   - "Your home just knows what you like"

4. **Peace of Mind**
   - Trust that safety and comfort are handled automatically
   - "Your family is protected—you'll know if anything's wrong"
   - "Energy optimizes itself while you're busy living"

## Empathy Statements

**On Smart Home Frustration:**
> "You're not crazy. Smart homes *have* been a mess. Seven apps, three voice assistants that don't talk to each other, automations that mysteriously stop working. We tried everything too. HeyZack is what we built after years of frustration."

**On Technical Intimidation:**
> "You don't need to be an engineer. If you can use a smartphone, you can set up HeyZack. Scan QR codes, follow the app, done in 30 minutes. Your non-tech partner will actually like this—promise."

**On Energy Anxiety:**
> "You want to save money and help the environment, but you're tired of being the 'energy police.' Nagging kids about lights doesn't work. HeyZack automates it—rooms turn off when empty, climate adjusts for occupancy. You save $30-70/month without thinking about it."

**On Security Overwhelm:**
> "Cameras showing you problems you can't solve. False alarms at 2 AM. Wondering if you actually locked the door. Security shouldn't create anxiety—it should eliminate it. HeyZack gives you actual control, not just notifications."

**On Platform Lock-In:**
> "You invested in Nest, then Google changed their strategy. Or Ring, then Amazon... you get it. We built HeyZack on open protocols (WiFi, Zigbee, Matter) so you're not trapped. Your data, your control, your choice to stay or leave."

## Copy Testing Checklist

Before finalizing B2C copy, verify:

- [ ] Uses "you" language (direct address to reader)
- [ ] Avoids technical jargon or explains it immediately
- [ ] Contains specific benefit or outcome (not vague promise)
- [ ] Acknowledges common frustration or pain point
- [ ] Sounds like a friend explaining, not a company pitching
- [ ] Includes proof point (savings amount, setup time, testimonial)
- [ ] Respects reader's intelligence without assuming expertise
- [ ] Passes the "would my non-tech partner understand this?" test
